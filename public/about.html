But what exactly are ancient grains? Ancient grains are a category of grains and pseudo-grains that have been cultivated for thousands of years, remaining largely unchanged by modern selective breeding techniques. These grains retain their unique flavors, nutritional profiles, and benefits. Compared to modern and refined grains, ancient grains are more nutrient-dense, rich in dietary fiber, vitamins (such as B vitamins and vitamin E), minerals (including magnesium, iron, and zinc), and antioxidants. They play crucial roles in various bodily functions, including energy production, immune function, and cell health. Additionally, ancient grains can grow properly without the aid of synthetic fertilizers due to their genetic adaptabilities and deep root systems.


In addition to ancient grains, our poundo incorporates vegetable roots, which are vegetables that grow underground and have edible roots or tubers. These root vegetables store energy and nutrients in their roots, making them rich sources of carbohydrates, fiber, vitamins, and minerals.


Let's delve into the ingredients that make up our Organic Nuden Poundo:


Ancient Grains Flours:

  • Khorasan: Also known as Kamut, this nutritious wheat variety is a good source of protein, dietary fiber, vitamins, and minerals. It promotes better digestion, brain function, provides energy, and is rich in antioxidants.

  • Spelt: Another wholesome wheat variety, spelt is rich in micronutrients, offering several health benefits such as improving heart health and reducing bloating.

  • Buckwheat: A gluten-free ancient grain-like seed, buckwheat is packed with essential amino acids, vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. It helps improve blood cholesterol levels, reduces the risk of heart disease, diabetes, stroke, and obesity.

  • Barley: This nutrient-dense grain is a good source of dietary fiber, particularly soluble fiber known as beta-glucan. It plays a significant role in fighting insulin resistance, cancer, dyslipidemia, hypertension, and obesity.

  • Amaranth: Another gluten-free ancient grain-like seed, amaranth is highly nutritious and rich in phosphorus, iron, and antioxidants. It supports brain function, bone health, blood production, and may reduce inflammation.

  • Sorghum: A nutrient-rich, gluten-free cereal grain, sorghum contains vitamins, minerals, and phenolic compounds that act as antioxidants. It promotes gut health and has anti-cancer effects.

  • Chickpea: Also known as garbanzo beans, chickpeas are an excellent source of plant-based protein, complex carbohydrates, vitamins, minerals, and dietary fiber. They support digestion, regulate blood sugar levels, aid in weight management, and contribute to fetal development during pregnancy.

Root Vegetables:

  • Potato flour: Made from dried potatoes, potato flour is gluten-free and rich in dietary fiber. It aids digestion, promotes regular bowel movements, and supports gut health. It also contains vitamins and minerals such as potassium, magnesium, and phosphorus.

  • Arrowroot powder: Derived from the arrowroot plant, this root vegetable is a source of protein and several minerals. It supports the immune system, aids weight loss, and provides essential folate for development during pregnancy.

  • Psyllium Husk: This dietary fiber is derived from the outer coating of Plantago ovata seeds. It is commonly used as a natural remedy for digestive issues and as a dietary supplement. Psyllium husk is low in carbs and calories, rich in minerals, vitamins, antioxidants, and has prebiotic effects.
